In Vivo Human Brain CEST MRI at 11.7T: First Results

Camélia Ressam Joseph Brégeat, Alfredo L. Lopez Kolkovsky, Alexis Amadon, Alexandre Vignaud, Aurélien Massire, Son Chu, Shajan Gunamony, Nicolas Boulant, Vincent Gras, Franck Mauconduit, Luisa Ciobanu.

ISMRM 2026, Cape Town, South Africa — Oral presentation

Motivation: The 11.7T whole-body MRI scanner offers new opportunities for advanced metabolic CEST imaging.
Goal: To assess the feasibility and performance of CEST imaging in the human brain at 11.7T.
Approach: CEST datasets were acquired in three participants using parallel transmission and virtual observation points to optimize saturation under SAR constraints. Multiple datasets targeted different CEST contrasts, and post-processing of residual B1+ inhomogeneities was applied. Slice-specific 3-spokes B1+ shimming and short saturation times were used to accommodate ultra-high-field limitations.
Results: CEST MTRasym maps showed clear gray-white matter differentiation across all acquisitions and participants. Post-processing correction of residual B1+ provided minimal improvement beyond pTx optimization.

CEST Perspectives for Human Brain Applications at 11.7 T

Camélia Ressam Vicent Gras, Franck Mauconduit, Luisa Ciobanu.

ISMRM 2025, Honolulu, Hawaii, USA — Poster

Motivation: High magnetic field CEST imaging presents unique opportunities for clinical applications development.
Goal(s): To investigate the potential of human brain CEST imaging on the 11.7 T scanner using tailored parallel transmission CEST pulses and compare performances with those of 7 T.
Approach: 7 T and 11.7 T B1+ maps were acquired on a realistic human head phantom and pTx CEST pulses were designed for a targeted B1+ value under different energy management strategies.
Results: Results: Results: Results: At 11.7 T, the optimized CEST pulses exhibit more inhomogeneity than at 7 T. pTx and VOPs are highly beneficial, with best homogeneity obtained for hydroxyl-weighted maps.
Impact: This study explores the potential of using the 11.7 T scanner for human brain CEST applications, addressing B1+ SAR compliance. It marks a first step toward clinical evaluation of CEST at magnetic fields higher than 7 T.

Characterization of Metabolic Changes in Healthy Aging Using High-Field CEST MRI

Thaddée Delebarre, Camélia Ressam, Vincent Gras, Franck Mauconduit, Alexandre Vignaud, Michel Bottlaender, Luisa Ciobanu.

EMIM 2024, Porto, Portugal — Poster

Chemical Exchange Saturation Transfer (CEST) MRI is a technique that can be used to study brain metabolism in a totally non-invasive manner and with improved spatial resolution and significantly higher sensitivity compared to magnetic resonance spectroscopy. Here, we take advantage of the improved CEST performance oﬀered by high magnetic fields (7T) to investigate the brain metabolic changes caused by healthy aging.
